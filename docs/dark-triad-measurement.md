# Dark Triad — Dirty Dozen (v1)

## Instrument
- **Name:** Dirty Dozen (Jonason & Webster, 2010)
- **Traits:** Machiavellianism (`MAC`), Psychopathy (`PSY`), Narcissism (`NAR`)
- **Items:** 12 (4 per trait)
- **App scale:** 1–5 Likert (original research often used 1–7)

## Item order (published)
1–4 Machiavellianism, 5–8 Psychopathy, 9–12 Narcissism. See `quiz-data/dark-triad-items.v1.json` for exact wording.

## Scoring
- No reverse-keyed items in the standard Dirty Dozen set.
- Trait mean = average of four keyed responses.
- Bands (editorial, same as Big Five short form): mean &lt; 2.5 → low; 2.5–3.5 → typical; &gt; 3.5 → high.
- Display percent = linear map of mean across 1–5 (not a population percentile).

## Use restrictions
- Informational self-report only. Not clinical.
- Do not use for hiring, admissions, or labeling others.
- Separate from the Big Five module; separate consent on intro.

## Quality
- Short forms are noisy; treat results as a rough snapshot.
- Replace band cutoffs after a local pilot if you collect responses.
